  • Comes from the author's imagination
  • Can include magical or impossible elements
  • Often has made-up characters and places
  • Uses descriptive language to paint pictures in our minds
  • Follows a narrative structure (beginning, middle, end)
  • Can teach us lessons through stories

Examples of fiction include:

  • Novels
  • Short stories
  • Fairy tales
  • Science fiction
  • Fantasy

ðŸ‘‰ Non-fiction:

  • Based on real facts, events, and people
  • Provides information about the real world
  • Uses verifiable details and sources
  • Often educational or informative in purpose
  • Can inspire us through the true stories of real people
  • Typically uses a more objective, factual tone

Examples of non-fiction include:

  • Biographies
  • History books
  • Science textbooks
  • Newspapers
  • How-to guides

Words that I learnt from the story

  • Firsthand: Learning or experiencing something directly, by yourself.
    Sentence: I learned firsthand how hard it is to take care of a pet when I got my new puppy.

  • Futuristic: Looking or sounding like it belongs in the future, often with advanced technology.
    Sentence: The futuristic car had features that seemed straight out of a science fiction movie.

  • Stumbled: To trip or lose balance while walking or moving.
    Sentence: While walking in the forest, I stumbled over a tree root and almost fell.
